# שפע יואל Skill

## IRON LAW #1 — DO NOT PUT WORDS IN THE REBBE'S MOUTH

**This is the most important rule in the entire skill. Every other rule is secondary to this one.**

Before writing ANY sentence in the top layer, ask: **"Did the Rebbe say this, or am I inventing it?"**

If you cannot point to the exact place in the source where the Rebbe says it — DO NOT WRITE IT. No matter how logical it sounds. No matter how much "stronger" it would make the point. No matter how obvious the inference seems.

**What counts as inventing:**
- Drawing a logical conclusion the Rebbe didn't draw ("if Torah elevates you, then without Torah you have no advantage" — HE didn't say that)
- Upgrading the Rebbe's language to something more dramatic ("עבירות חמורות" → "עומקי השפלות עד תכליתה" — HE didn't say that)
- Extracting a message from a single word ("כושל" → "don't feel bad if you fell" — HE didn't say that)
- Adding a "knife twist" closer that sounds powerful but isn't in the source

**What to do instead:**
- Use the Rebbe's OWN words and phrases
- Stay within what HE explicitly said
- If you want to make it stronger, find ANOTHER source that says what you want to add — don't invent it
- When in doubt, leave it out

**This rule applies even when the user asks to "make it stronger."** Making it stronger means finding the bombshell WITHIN the source, not adding one from outside.

---

## IRON LAW #2 — SUBTRACT, DON'T ADD

**Every word that doesn't hit harder makes everything hit softer.**

The instinct is always to ADD — more adjectives, more qualifiers, more clauses, more weight. FIGHT THIS INSTINCT. The skill is to SUBTRACT.

**Concrete rules:**
- If a simpler word says the same thing, use the simpler word: `ונמצאים עד היום` beats `וממשן וכח קדושתם נמצאים עדיין`
- If something was already said, don't repeat it. If `קול תורה ותפלה` was mentioned above, don't say it again.
- If a qualifier adds no new meaning, cut it: `יש הרגש לכל אחד` beats `שטיקל הרגשה יש לכל אחד מישראל`
- `עד היום` (concrete, present) beats `עדיין` (weak, vague)
- Cut every `הקדושות`, `מישראל`, and other trailing qualifiers that the reader already knows from context

**The test before submitting:** Read every sentence and ask: "Can I say this in fewer words without losing meaning?" If yes — cut. A 13-year-old bochur should never stumble on a sentence. If he has to read it twice, it's too heavy.

**BUT REMEMBER: Iron Law #2 is OVERRIDDEN by the one-two punch rule.** Before cutting ANYTHING, ask: "Is this saying the SAME thing as what's next to it, or a DIFFERENT thing?" If different — DO NOT CUT even if it looks redundant. Example: `נמצאים עד היום באויר העולם` (WHERE — they're here) + `נצחיים וקיימים לעד` (HOW LONG — forever). Looks like repetition. It's not. One is the tape in the recorder. The other is the tape still playing. Together = the bombshell. Apart = nothing special. THIS MISTAKE WAS MADE EVEN AFTER THE RULE EXISTED. Always pause and think before cutting.

**This is not about being brief. It's about being SHARP.** A short sentence that hits is better than a long sentence that impresses. The goal is a missile, not a parade.

---

## IRON LAW #3 — WHEN ASKED TO COMBINE, DO NOT ADD

**When the user gives a clean version and asks to combine or strengthen it with source material, the answer is almost always: the user's version is the top layer, the source material stays in the footnote.**

The instinct is to "contribute" by inserting phrases from the source into the user's clean text. FIGHT THIS. The user already extracted the punchline. The details you want to add are exactly what he already decided to leave in the footnote.

**Process when asked to "combine":**
1. Read the user's version
2. Read the source material
3. Ask: "Is there something in the source that the user MISSED that changes the message?" — not adds detail, not adds beauty, but changes the ACTUAL MESSAGE
4. If NO (which is almost always the case) — the user's version is ready. Say so. Don't touch it.
5. If YES — propose ONLY the missing message, not the surrounding language

**The user's clean version is not a draft for you to improve. It's the finished product that you need to recognize as finished.**

## What This Skill Does

Takes raw Torah material (quotes from Divrei Yoel, Divros Kodesh, Kedushas Yoel, etc.) and converts it into the **שער התורה two-layer format**:

- **TOP** = Actionable headlines + motivational numbered paragraphs (what YOU need to do)
- **BOTTOM** = Full original Torah sources in footnotes (the raw material, untouched)

This is NOT a translation. It's a TRANSFORMATION. The top tells you what to do with your life. The bottom is where the Torah lives.

---

## The Complete Process (Step by Step)

### Step 1: Analyze the Raw Material

Read every word of the source. Ask yourself:
1. What are the **actionable takeaways** a Yid can take from this?
2. What is telling me something to DO vs. something historical/contextual?
3. How many separate points are here?

**Critical Rule**: Only things that are GUIDELINES, RULES, or PUNCHLINES become top-layer points. Historical observations, background context, and tangential explanations stay in the footnotes only.

### Step 2: Draft the Points

**FIRST: Before drafting individual points, look across ALL sources and ask: "Do any of these sources ESCALATE each other?"** If Source B intensifies Source A's claim (same theme, more extreme angle, "especially in our generation," removes alternatives), COMBINE them into one escalating point using `ובפרט` as the hinge. Two medium-strength points are weaker than one devastating crescendo. Key signals to combine:
- Source B addresses the same theme from a more extreme angle
- Source B says "especially in our generation" or "especially now"
- Source B removes alternatives (fasting, korbanos, etc.) leaving only Torah
- Source B would feel weaker standing alone without Source A's foundation

For each actionable takeaway, draft:

**a. The Bold Headline** (5-15 words, ends with `:`)
- Must be actionable. Ask: "If someone reads ONLY this line, do they know what to do?"
- Use direct address: `ואל יפול לבו`, `ישית אל לבו`, `יזהר מאד`, `אל יחשוב בלבו`
- Examples of GOOD headlines:
  - `ואל יפול לבו לומר מה יועיל כח התורה המועט שלי:`
  - `כשידע זאת לא יפסיק מלימודו לעולם:`
  - `לא יחסוך כל ממונו לקנות התורה:`
- Examples of BAD headlines (too vague, too historical):
  - `ענין חטא העגל ושבירת הלוחות:` (historical, not actionable)
  - `דברים נפלאים בענין כח התורה:` (vague, says nothing)

**b. The Numbered Paragraph** (2-5 sentences)

This is where the style matters most. There are TWO types of paragraphs:

**TYPE A — Knocking down a doubt:**
Use when the source addresses a person's potential excuse or discouragement.

1. **Open by naming the person's inner voice / excuse / feeling**:
   - `ואל יפול לבו של אדם לומר...`
   - `ואל יאמר בלבו...`
   - `ואל יחשוב לומר...`
2. **State the excuse/doubt clearly**
3. **Knock it down** — introduced by `ישית אל לבו, כי`, `כי דוקא`, `כי באמת` (AVOID `ידע` — too cerebral)
4. **Land the punchline**

**TYPE B — Positive statement about Torah's power:**
Use when the source is stating a fact about Torah's greatness or power. NOT every paragraph needs to address a doubt. Sometimes the point is simply: "Torah is so powerful that X."

1. **Open with the positive fact**: `גדול עד מאד...`, `כח התורה...`, `עצום וגדול הוא...`
2. **Build upward** — each clause adds another layer
3. **Peak with the strongest claim** — `ואפילו... יש לו תקנה ע"י כח התורה`

**CRITICAL: Use the source's own words.** Do NOT write poetry or dramatic language that isn't in the source. If the source says `עבירות חמורות`, use `עבירות חמורות`. Don't upgrade it to `עומקי השפלות עד תכליתה`. If the source says `תקנה ע"י כח התורה`, use that. Don't rewrite it as `מגיע עד למקומות הנמוכים ביותר ויכול להוציאו ולהעלותו`. Stay close to how the Rebbe said it.

**CRITICAL: Find the BOMBSHELL, not the summary.** Every source has a punchline that hits like a missile. Your job is to find it and put it on top. The bombshell is usually a CONTRAST — something shockingly powerful set against something terrifyingly destructive. For example: if the source discusses the dor hamabul, the bombshell is NOT "Torah brings teshuva" (that's weak and generic). The bombshell IS: Hashem destroyed the entire world because of those sins, and Torah is so powerful that those same sins — you have a tikun. The thing that ended creation cannot end you because you have Torah. THAT'S the missile. Always ask: "What is the most powerful contrast or claim in this source?" That's what goes on top.

**CRITICAL: Keep the subject right.** If the section is about כח התורה, the subject is TORAH. Don't flip it to be about the person's suffering or fall. If the source says "Torah can fix even the worst sins," lead with Torah's power, not with the person's lowliness.

**The paragraph must BREATHE.** It's not a compressed summary. It flows like speech, like a father talking to his son. Use words like:
- `דוקא` (specifically, precisely)
- `כל מעט ומעט` (every little bit)
- `חשוב ויקר מאד` (double emphasis)
- `ובזכות אותו המעט` (ties back)

**Language Rules:**
- Elevated Lashon Hakodesh ONLY. No modern Hebrew.
- Use: `ואל יפול לבו`, `ישית אל לבו`, `ישריש בנפשו`, `צא ולמד`, `אשרי מי ש`, `ולפיכך`
- Connect ideas with: `וכן`, `ולא זו בלבד`, `ולפיכך`, `ואף על פי כן`, `אבל`, `ומכל מקום`
- NEVER: `בהחלט`, `למעשה`, `בעצם`, `פשוט`, or any modern Hebrew filler

### Step 3: Determine Placement

If inserting into an existing chapter:
1. Read the full chapter structure
2. Find WHERE the new point fits thematically
3. Check if the existing text already hints at the topic (like point 6 ending with `וגם הגאולה העתידה תלויה בזכות התורה`)
4. Insert after the natural lead-in
5. Renumber everything that follows

### Step 4: Prepare the Footnotes

Each footnote contains:
- **Citation**: `דברי יואל (פרשה עמ' xxx):` — exact sefer, parsha, page
- **Full quote**: In original language (Hebrew for Divrei Yoel, Yiddish for Divros Kodesh)
- **Supporting sources**: Prefixed with `@` on a new line: `@ובדברי יואל (פרשה עמ' xxx):`
- **Context from the raw material stays here** — historical observations, tangential explanations, the Korach digression, etc.

### Step 5: Confirm with the User

Before writing the final docx:
1. Present the drafted points (headlines + paragraphs)
2. Explain where they would be inserted
3. Get approval or feedback
4. Iterate until the user says it hits right

### Step 6: Produce the Document

When creating the .docx, read `/mnt/skills/public/docx/SKILL.md` for technical instructions, then follow the formatting specs in `references/formatting.md`.

## Filtering Rules: What Goes on Top vs. What Stays Below

### TOP LAYER (bold headline + numbered paragraph):
- ✅ Direct instructions: "Do this", "Don't do that"
- ✅ Motivational truths: "Know that X is true, therefore Y"
- ✅ Practical consequences: "If you do X, you get Y"
- ✅ Warnings: "If ח"ו you don't do X, then Z happens"
- ✅ Encouragement against discouragement: "Don't think X, because Y"

### BOTTOM LAYER ONLY (footnotes):
- ❌ Historical events (what happened with Korach, the Egel, etc.) — unless the lesson is actionable
- ❌ Explanations of pesukim that are purely academic
- ❌ Long pilpul or back-and-forth between sources
- ❌ Background context needed to understand a quote
- ❌ Tangential observations the Rebbe made within a longer Torah
- ❌ **Drashos and Torah insights** — e.g. "the angels didn't need Torah because they have no yetzer hara" is a DRASHA. It explains a sugya. The top layer doesn't explain Torah. It tells you what to DO with it. The drasha belongs in the footnote. The top layer extracts ONLY the actionable punchline.

**CRITICAL RULE: Do NOT turn source material into drashos in the top layer.** If the source discusses angels vs. humans, the egel, Korach's mistake, or any Torah concept — the EXPLANATION stays in the footnote. The top layer takes ONLY the practical consequence. For example:
- Source discusses why Torah was given to Israel and not angels → Top layer says: "Torah is the only weapon against the yetzer hara, without it you cannot win"
- Source discusses Korach's error → That's context. It stays in the footnote only.
- Source explains a Gemara → The explanation is the footnote. The top layer extracts: what does this mean for how I live?

**CRITICAL RULE: Do NOT invent messages that aren't in the source.** If the source says "ינתנו לכושל שבהם", don't turn that into "don't feel bad if you fell." Only extract what the source ACTUALLY says. Do not read INTO the source.

**CRITICAL RULE: An `@` source can feed the top layer.** Do NOT automatically treat `@` supporting sources as footnote-only material. Before demoting an `@` source to footnote-only, ask: **"Does this source carry the bombshell?"** If the `@` source contains the most powerful, concrete, visceral version of the point — it belongs on top. For example: if the main source says the voices of Sinai are "נצחיות" (abstract), but the `@` source says "בקעו את האויר וממשן מונח באויר" (concrete, physical, visceral) — the `@` source is carrying the punchline. USE IT on top. The `@` marker only means it's a supporting citation, NOT that its content is secondary. Judge by the CONTENT, not by the `@` label.

**The test**: If it doesn't answer "What should I DO?" or "What should I KNOW that changes how I act?" — it stays in the footnotes.

## Quality Checklist

Before presenting to the user, verify:

- [ ] **IRON LAW: Every sentence can be traced to something the Rebbe actually said** — no invented inferences, no upgraded language, no logical conclusions he didn't draw
- [ ] **IRON LAW #2: Every sentence has been trimmed.** Read each sentence and ask: "Can I say this in fewer words?" Cut every qualifier that adds no meaning. Cut every repetition. Replace heavy phrases with simple ones. If a bochur has to read it twice, it's too heavy.
- [ ] Every bold headline is actionable (not vague, not historical)
- [ ] Every numbered paragraph flows naturally (not compressed, not stiff)
- [ ] The paragraph "breathes" — has build-up, a turn, and a punchline
- [ ] Language is elevated Lashon Hakodesh (no modern Hebrew)
- [ ] Tone is direct and commanding, like a father to his son
- [ ] Footnotes contain the FULL original source, unedited
- [ ] Footnote citations are exact (sefer, parsha, page)
- [ ] Supporting sources use `@` prefix
- [ ] Placement in existing chapter makes logical sense
- [ ] The new points flow naturally from what comes before and into what comes after
- [ ] Numbers are adjusted correctly after insertion
